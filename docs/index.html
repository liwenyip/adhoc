Australia’s vast geographical expanses often present challenging communications scenarios. In many cases, ad hoc networking can provide a more flexible and economical alternative to traditional solutions.

The JCU Remote Ad Hoc Networks project is focused on developing hardware and software suitable for these scenarios, which are characterised by remote and inaccessible locations, large geographical areas, sparse topologies, and link ranges of up to 10 kilometres.
